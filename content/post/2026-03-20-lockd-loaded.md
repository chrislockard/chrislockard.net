- [I traced $2 billion in nonprofit grants and 45 states of lobbying records to
  figure out who's behind the age verification bills. The answer involves a
  company that profits from your data writing laws that collect more of it. :
  linux][1] - I love effective citizen journalism.


- [Ghostty 1.3.1 release][2] - This release fixes some macOS-specific issues.
  I've been a huge Ghostty fan since it hit general availability, and just
  yesterday, ran `brew uninstall --cask iterm2`.

- [The 49MB Web Page | thatshubham][3] - The web's bloat is real. I often miss
  the simple days of HTML with a little CSS, tables, and iframes. The web felt
  faster back then. Sure it looks better in some ways today, but 49MB for a web
  page that's mostly ads and trackers? Get outta here.

- [Web Design Museum: Naptser in 2000][4] - Speaking of the good 'ol days,
  here's what a famous website at the turn of the millennium looked like.

- [Digg is gone again][5] - I had signed up for beta access a few months ago and
  spent a couple hours here and there. It felt like it was slowly gainin
  adoption, but the AI/bot problem is real. Is the web just doomed to this
  spammic future?

- [Say hello to MacBook Neo - Apple][6] - After using one for a week, I'm highly
  impressed at how capable this little thing is. The education and entry-level
  markets are obvious, but this is a *very* compelling device for people who
  want a mobile computing experience but don't like how nerfed iPads or other
  tablets are. The Neo feels *so good* to carry around and use. It's like bringing the joy of the desktop battlestation with me wherever I go.

That's all for this week! God bless.
