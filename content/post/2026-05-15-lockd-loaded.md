- [Obsidian Community][obsidian] - Obsidian.md, the personal knowledge
  management app I've used for several years released a great community site.
  This site allows one to review the security health of their favorite plugins.
  I scrutinized and removed nearly a dozen plugins based on their subpar
  security scores. I want to see more tools like Obsidian community.

- [Apfel - Interact With Your Mac's Built-In LLM][apfel] - Apple Foundation
  Models are typically only exposed via code or through Apple Intelligence.
  Apfel allows users to chat with these models - built into all macs, iPhones,
  and iPads with Apple Intelligence - and even set up OpenAI-compatible
  endpoints for powering other tools. The model is anemic by some standards and
  has a very small context window of 4096, but it is executed on Apple Silicon's
  NPUs! 

- [Getting Started with Apple Foundation Models][getstartedwithafm] - Excellent
  blog post and tutorial about using Apple Foundation Models from Swift. 

- [Fark.com][fark] - If you've been online as long as I have, you'll almost
  certainly have run across Fark. I was reminded of it this week while browsing
  Hackernews and visited the site for the first time in years.  In a sea of AI slop, Fark is re-emerging as an island of human creativity and expression.

That's all for this week! God bless.
